Essential Lessons

⊕

Order of Operations

  • Parentheses first
  • Exponents next
  • Multiply & Divide (L→R)
  • Add & Subtract (L→R)
3 + 4 × 2 = 3 + 8 = 11
∶

Fractions & Percentages

  • Add fractions → find LCD first
  • % = (part ÷ whole) × 100
¾ + ⅔ = 9/12 + 8/12 = 17/12 % = (part / whole) × 100
𝕡

Prime Numbers

  • Exactly 2 factors: 1 and itself
  • Test divisibility up to √n
  • Every integer > 1 has unique prime factorization
360 = 2³ × 3² × 5
∩

GCD & LCM

GCD: largest shared factor. LCM: smallest shared multiple.

GCD(a,b) = GCD(b, a mod b) LCM(a,b) = |a·b| / GCD(a,b)
μ

Mean · Median · Mode

  • Mean: Σx ÷ n
  • Median: middle value (sorted)
  • Mode: most frequent value
  • Range: max − min
Mean{2,4,6,8} = 20/4 = 5
⊢

Propositional Logic

  • ¬P — NOT  |  P ∧ Q — AND
  • P ∨ Q — OR
  • P → Q — false only if P=T, Q=F
  • P ↔ Q — same truth value
P → Q ≡ ¬P ∨ Q
∈

Set Theory

  • A ∪ B — union (A or B)
  • A ∩ B — intersection (both)
  • A − B — difference
  • A △ B — symmetric difference
|A∪B| = |A| + |B| − |A∩B|
ₙCᵣ

Combinatorics

  • P(n,r) = n!/(n−r)! — ordered
  • C(n,r) = n!/[r!(n−r)!] — unordered
C(5,2) = 10  |  P(5,2) = 20
